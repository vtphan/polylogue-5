Step 1: Project Idea
About This Section:


This section provides a “big picture” overview of the project idea aligned to specific learning goals for the focus grade level and content area. It also includes hyperlinks to key project resources.
	Considerations
* Consider Your Content: What content and skills do you need to teach in the coming months? How might you need to adjust the learning goals, driving questions, and products so that the project can be a vehicle for students to learn this content and skills?
* Consider Your Students: What adjustments might you need to make to connect this project with your students’ interests, identities, and passions?
* Consider Your Context: What resources and/or challenges in your own community might help to make this project more authentic for students?
	





Driving Question
	How do our senses influence our actions and decisions, both now and in the future?
	Standards Addressed
	Science Standards:
   * 7.LS1.5: 
      * Obtain and communicate information to provide evidence that illustrates the causal relationships between information received by sensory receptors and behavior, both immediate and over longer time scales.
Math Standards:
   * 7.RP.A.1 (Unit Rates):
      * Calculate the rate of a specific response to a sensory input (e.g., heartbeats per minute, frequency of a reflex per trial).
      * Use these unit rates to quantify the impact of different senses.
   * 7.RP.A.2 (Proportional Relationships):
      * Create tables and graphs in your Sensory Log to show relationships between sensory input and behavioral output.
      * Identify if relationships are proportional and determine the constant of proportionality.
      * Use graphs in your Design Portfolio to illustrate predicted outcomes.
   * 7.RP.A.3 (Multi-step Ratio & Percent Problems):
      * Report findings using percentages (e.g., "80% of participants felt calm").
      * Use proportions to scale your prototype to real-world dimensions for your design.
	Project Summary
	As "Sensory Superhero" designers and engineers, you will research how sensory inputs lead to behaviors, then design and present a product or space that uses senses to achieve a specific goal.


	Major Product(s)
	Group Products
	Students will build a physical prototype, digital presentation or detailed drawing of their solution to a sensory-related goal
	Making It Public
	Presentation of prototype to community members.


	







Step 2: Project Path Overview




Project Launch (1 Friday)
	Milestone
	Guidance
	Learning Experiences                                                                (Lessons, Activities, and Scaffolds)
	Entry event: Sensory Station Exploration




	Students will focus on what they feel, hear, see, smell, and how their body responds.
	* Students in nine groups of five participate in a sensory station exploration (rotation), cycling through experiences of the senses of touch, taste/smell, hearing, and vision.
* Students complete a closing reflection on the following topics: reliability of various senses, how teamwork helped or made the activity more difficult, and how people perceive the same thing differently.
* Final questions: Which part of this project are you most excited to explore? Do you have any initial ideas for problems you’d like to solve with sensory design?
