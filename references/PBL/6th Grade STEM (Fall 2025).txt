Step 1: Project Idea
About This Section:


This section provides a “big picture” overview of the project idea aligned to specific learning goals for the focus grade level and content area. It also includes hyperlinks to key project resources.
	Considerations
* Consider Your Content: What content and skills do you need to teach in the coming months? How might you need to adjust the learning goals, driving questions, and products so that the project can be a vehicle for students to learn this content and skills?
* Consider Your Students: What adjustments might you need to make to connect this project with your students’ interests, identities, and passions?
* Consider Your Context: What resources and/or challenges in your own community might help to make this project more authentic for students?
	



Driving Question
	What are the major threats affecting our global environment, and what can our communities do to protect our ecosystems?
	Standards Addressed
	Science Standards:
   * 6.LS24, 6.LS2.6, 6.LS4.1, 6.ESS3.1, 6.ESS3.2, 6.ESS3.3, 6.ETS1.1
Math Standards:
   * 6.SP.A.1, 6.SP.B.4,  
	Project Summary
	Students are exploring environmental climate issues. As a group, they are picking one specific issue, researching all aspects, and creating a final product to raise awareness to the general public. Through this STEM PBL, students will become experts on their environmental issue through research and exploration. They will then 


	Major Product(s)
	                                                                                Group Products
	Students will create a digital presentation that summarizes their research and design an escape room.
	Making It Public
	Presentation of group-specific topics and audience completion of Escape Rooms

	* Form project teams. Create team norms and working agreements.
* Complete and debrief the Cooler Design Challenge.
